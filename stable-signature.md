# What is an AI Image Watermark?

## What Stegmarc found
Stegmarc detected a Stable Signature watermark — Meta AI's system for identifying images generated by Stable Diffusion AI image generators.

## Why AI companies watermark their images
Major AI companies are required (or choosing proactively) to label AI-generated images. The EU AI Act mandates disclosure of AI-generated content. Meta, Adobe, Google, and others have committed to watermarking their AI outputs as part of responsible AI deployment.

## What Stable Signature does
Instead of adding a watermark after the image is generated, Stable Signature modifies the AI model itself to subtly embed a 48-bit identifier into every image it produces. This means:
- Every image from a watermarked model carries the identifier automatically
- The watermark survives common image manipulations
- The identifier can be traced back to the specific model that generated the image

## What this means for you

**If you're a compliance officer:** This image was generated by an AI system using Meta's Stable Signature watermarking. The bit pattern decoded from the image can identify the specific model version. This is relevant for EU AI Act compliance, copyright disputes, and content authenticity verification.

**If you're a business owner:** If this fires on an image you didn't generate with AI, it may indicate your image was replaced or modified by an AI system downstream.
